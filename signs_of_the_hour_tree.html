Signs of the Hour (Ashrāṭ as-Sāʿah) — A Chronological Tree

The portents of the Day of Judgement in the Sunni Islamic tradition, arranged in their commonly understood order — the minor signs (aṣ-ṣughrā) first, beginning in the Prophet's own lifetime and unfolding through history, leading to the major signs (al-kubrā), which follow one another in close succession at the very end of time. Designed for printing — use A3 landscape, or Save as PDF.

This chart is a study aid summarising the traditional accounts drawn from the Qurʾān and the ḥadīth collections (especially Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī and Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim). The exact timing and order — particularly among the minor signs — is a matter of scholarly interpretation (ijtihād), not certainty; only Allah knows the Hour (Qurʾān 7:187). The grouping below reflects when each sign is generally said to begin to appear.

I · Minor Signs Already Passed — From the Prophet's Lifetime Onward
The Sending of the Prophet ﷺ ★
610 – 632 CE · Mecca & Medina
The First & Foremost Sign
The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said, "I was sent, and the Hour, like these two" — holding up two fingers (Bukhārī, Muslim). His prophethood, being the final messenger, is itself the opening sign that the end of time has drawn near. Every later sign descends from this point.
Death of the Prophet ﷺ
11 AH / 632 CE
Foretold Sign
Named explicitly among the signs in the ḥadīth of ʿAwf ibn Mālik (Bukhārī): "Count six signs before the Hour: the first is my death."
Conquest of Jerusalem
16 AH / 637 CE
Foretold Sign
The second of the six signs in the same ḥadīth — the opening of Bayt al-Maqdis (Jerusalem) under ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb.
The Plague of ʿAmwās
18 AH / 639 CE
Foretold Sign
A mass mortality ("like the murrain of sheep") foretold as the third sign; a great plague struck Syria at ʿAmwās in the time of the Companions.
Civil Strife & Wealth
35 AH onward
Fitan & Surplus
The assassination of ʿUthmān, the first fitna, and wealth so abundant that "a man given 100 dīnār is still discontent" — further signs from the ḥadīth of the six.
The Liars Claiming Prophethood
11 AH onward
~30 False Prophets
"The Hour will not come until about thirty liars, each claiming to be a messenger of Allah, appear" (Bukhārī, Muslim). Began with Musaylimah and al-Aswad in the Prophet's own time and continued through history.
Loss of the Trustworthy & Knowledge
Early generations onward
Removal of ʿIlm
Knowledge is taken by the death of the scholars; ignorant leaders give verdicts without knowledge, "straying and leading others astray" (Bukhārī).
Fire of the Ḥijāz
654 AH / 1256 CE
Foretold Sign
"Fire will come out of the land of Ḥijāz which will light up the necks of the camels in Buṣrā" (Bukhārī, Muslim) — historically identified with the volcanic eruption near Medina in 1256 CE.
II · Minor Signs Unfolding — Moral & Social Decline
When Trust (Amānah) is Lost ★
Ongoing
Affairs Given to the Unworthy
A Bedouin asked when the Hour would be; the Prophet ﷺ said, "When the trust is lost, await the Hour" — "when authority is given to those unfit for it" (Bukhārī). The master theme uniting the social signs of decline below.
The Slave-Woman's Master
Ongoing
Ḥadīth of Jibrīl
"The slave-woman will give birth to her mistress/master" — variously read as the collapse of family ties, children disrespecting parents, or vast social reversal (Muslim).
Barefoot Shepherds Build High
Ongoing
Ḥadīth of Jibrīl
"The barefoot, naked, destitute shepherds competing in constructing tall buildings" — the poor becoming rich and vying in skyscrapers (Bukhārī, Muslim).
Spread of Riba, Zinā & Wine
Ongoing
Open Sin
Usury becomes widespread, adultery and intoxicants are openly practised and even named by other names — recurring portents in the ḥadīth literature.
Music, Killing & Time Contracting
Ongoing
Disorder
Prevalence of singing/instruments, frequent killing (al-harj) where killer and killed know not why, and "time passing quickly" so a year feels like a month (Bukhārī, Aḥmad).
Weakening of Religion ★
Ongoing
Faith Held Like Hot Coal
"There will come a time when holding to one's religion is like holding a burning coal" (Tirmidhī). Islam becomes strange again, as it began — practised firmly by few.
Greeting Only the Known
Ongoing
Erosion of Brotherhood
"A man will greet (with salām) only those he knows" — the loss of universal Muslim brotherhood and trade overtaking ties of kinship (Aḥmad).
Truthful Disbelieved, Liar Believed
Ongoing
The Years of Deceit
"Years of deceit in which the truthful is disbelieved, the liar believed, the trustworthy called a traitor, and the treacherous trusted; and the Ruwaybiḍah will speak" — the foolish nobody addressing public affairs (Ibn Mājah, Aḥmad).
III · Later Minor Signs — Approaching the End
Great Wars & Upheavals ★
Approaching the End
Fitan Before the Major Signs
A cluster of momentous events the ḥadīths place near (but before) the major signs — widespread tribulations that prepare the stage for the Mahdī and Dajjāl.
Euphrates Uncovers a Mountain of Gold
Future
Bukhārī & Muslim
"The Hour will not come until the Euphrates uncovers a mountain of gold over which people fight, ninety-nine of every hundred dying." A warning not to take from it.
Arabia: Meadows & Rivers Again
Future
Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim
"The Hour will not come until the land of the Arabs once more becomes meadows and rivers" — a reversal of the desert.
Conquest of Constantinople / Rome
Future (near the Dajjāl)
A Final Great Conquest
A great army of believers conquers the city by takbīr, not weapons, just before the appearance of the Dajjāl (Muslim) — distinguished by scholars from the 1453 conquest.
The Great Battle (al-Malḥamah)
Future (near the Dajjāl)
Massive War in al-Shām
A tremendous war and the truce-then-betrayal foretold in Muslim, immediately preceding the emergence of the Dajjāl — the hinge between the minor and major signs.
Signs at the Threshold ★
Just Before the Major Signs
The Last of the Minor Signs
Portents the tradition places right at the brink — the final indicators that the major signs are about to begin in succession.
Abundance of Wealth — No Takers
Future
Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim
Wealth so abundant that a man offered charity finds no one to accept it — associated with the just rule near the end (and the time of ʿĪsā).
Speaking Inanimate Objects
Future
Tirmidhī
"A man's whip or the thong of his sandal will speak to him, and his thigh will tell him what his people did after him" — a sign of nearness often linked to later technology by commentators.
IV · Major Signs (al-Kubrā) — The Beginning
Imām al-Mahdī ★
First of the great figures
The Rightly-Guided Leader
A man from the Prophet's family (descended from Fāṭimah), named Muhammad ibn ʿAbdillāh, who fills the earth with justice as it was filled with injustice, ruling for about seven years (Abū Dāwūd, Tirmidhī). His era overlaps the emergence of the Dajjāl and the descent of ʿĪsā. (Reported in Sunni sources; the Mahdī also holds central but differently-detailed importance in Shīʿī thought.)
Al-Masīḥ ad-Dajjāl ★
The greatest trial
The False Messiah / Antichrist
A one-eyed deceiver with "Kāfir" written between his eyes, who claims divinity and roams the earth in forty days (one like a year, one like a month, one like a week), bringing false "paradise" and "hell." The greatest fitna since Adam; barred only from Mecca and Medina (Muslim). Every prophet warned of him.
Descent of ʿĪsā (Jesus) ﷺ ★
During the Dajjāl's reign
The Return of the Messiah
Jesus son of Mary descends by the white minaret east of Damascus, breaks the cross, abolishes the jizyah, prays behind the Mahdī, and kills the Dajjāl at the gate of Ludd (Lod). He rules with justice; wealth overflows and prostration becomes better than the world (Bukhārī, Muslim).
V · Major Signs — Cosmic Upheaval
Yaʾjūj wa Maʾjūj (Gog & Magog) ★
In the time of ʿĪsā ﷺ
The Released Hordes
After the Dajjāl's death, the barrier of Dhū al-Qarnayn (Qurʾān 18:94–99, 21:96) is breached and Gog and Magog pour forth "from every height," drinking lakes dry and spreading corruption. ʿĪsā and the believers take refuge on Mount Ṭūr; Allah destroys the hordes with worms in their necks, and birds clear the earth of them (Muslim).
The Three Landslides (Khasf)
Among the ten major signs
Earth Swallowing
Three great sinkings of the earth — one in the East, one in the West, and one in the Arabian Peninsula — listed among the ten major signs in the ḥadīth of Ḥudhayfah ibn Asīd (Muslim).
The Smoke (ad-Dukhān)
Among the ten major signs
Qurʾān 44:10–11
"Watch for the Day when the sky brings a visible smoke covering the people — a painful punishment." Counted among the ten major signs; it afflicts the whole earth, the believer touched as by a cold, the disbeliever overwhelmed.
VI · Major Signs — The Final Sequence & the Hour
Sunrise From the West ★
The door of repentance closes
The Sun Rising From its Setting-place
"The Hour will not come until the sun rises from the west; when people see it, all will believe — but on that Day, faith will not benefit a soul that did not believe before" (Bukhārī, Muslim; cf. Qurʾān 6:158). After this, the gate of repentance (tawbah) is shut forever.
The Beast of the Earth (ad-Dābbah)
Near the sunrise from the west
Qurʾān 27:82
"We will bring forth from the earth a Beast that will speak to them" — it marks the faces of believer and disbeliever, distinguishing them. Said in the ḥadīth to emerge close in time to the sun's rising from the west (Muslim).
The Fire That Gathers Mankind ★
The last of the ten signs
From Yemen / ʿAdan
"The last of the signs is a fire that comes out of Yemen (from the depths of ʿAdan) and drives the people to their place of gathering (al-maḥshar)" — the final sign in the list of Ḥudhayfah ibn Asīd (Muslim).
The Blowing of the Trumpet ★
The Hour itself
Isrāfīl & the Ṣūr
The Hour arrives: Isrāfīl blows the Trumpet (Qurʾān 39:68, 69:13). At the first blast all in the heavens and earth fall dead; at the second, all are resurrected for the Day of Judgement. Beyond this lies not a "sign" but the Reckoning itself.

Cross-Links — How the Signs Relate

  • The Prophet ﷺ "and the Hour like these two": his sending is the first sign, so the entire chain of minor signs is reckoned from his lifetime onward.
  • Minor → Major in order of magnitude: the tradition consistently teaches that the many minor signs (ṣughrā) appear gradually over history, while the major signs (kubrā) come close together at the end "like beads of a necklace whose string is cut."
  • Moral decline ↔ loss of trust (amānah): nearly all the social signs — unfit leaders, open sin, the disbelieved-truthful, the speaking Ruwaybiḍah — are facets of the single ḥadīth "when trust is lost, await the Hour."
  • The Great Battle (Malḥamah) → the Dajjāl: the wars in al-Shām and the conquest of Constantinople are the last minor signs, forming the immediate bridge into the major signs.
  • Mahdī ↔ ʿĪsā ↔ Dajjāl — one interlocking drama: the Mahdī leads, ʿĪsā descends during the Dajjāl's reign and prays behind the Mahdī, and it is ʿĪsā who kills the Dajjāl at Ludd.
  • Dajjāl's death → release of Gog & Magog: the breaching of Dhū al-Qarnayn's barrier follows directly upon the Dajjāl's defeat, during the rule of ʿĪsā.
  • The "Ten Major Signs" of Ḥudhayfah ibn Asīd (Muslim): the Smoke, the Dajjāl, the Beast, sunrise from the west, the descent of ʿĪsā, Gog & Magog, the three landslides (East, West, Arabia), and the Fire — the canonical checklist this chart follows.
  • Sunrise from the west → the closing of repentance: this cosmic reversal ends the age of accepted faith; thereafter belief no longer benefits the one who waited.
  • The Beast ↔ the sun from the west: the ḥadīth pairs these two as near-simultaneous, "whichever comes first, the other follows closely."
  • The Fire of gathering → the Trumpet: the last of the ten signs herds humanity to the place of assembly, after which Isrāfīl's Trumpet ends the worldly order and opens the Resurrection.
  • Qurʾān ↔ ḥadīth: several major signs are named in the Qurʾān (the Smoke 44:10, the Beast 27:82, Gog & Magog 18 & 21, the sun's belated faith 6:158, the Trumpet 39:68) and detailed in the Sunnah.
  • "Only Allah knows the Hour" (7:187, 31:34): the signs announce nearness, never a date — the framing principle for reading this entire chart.
